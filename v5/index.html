I. The Operator

A man with a thousand leads and a clipboard.

The small business owner I am thinking about runs a residential service company in Las Vegas. He has a truck, a route, and a small spiral notebook that smells faintly of sun. On any given morning he is doing the work of five people: dispatcher, accountant, marketer, technician, and the human who answers the phone before nine.

He is good at his job. He is even better at being relentless. What he is not good at, by his own admission, is keeping up. His phone holds one thousand leads he never called back. His ad account holds about four thousand five hundred dollars of spend he cannot trace to a single signed customer. His week holds thirteen hours of repeat administrative work that he does because no one else will.

This is a story about that operator and about the quiet, unglamorous category of software that, properly aimed, gives him those thirteen hours back.

II. The Audit

What an AI Business Assessment actually is.

Strip away the language. An AI Business Assessment is a structured look at where your business already produces friction, multiplied by a working knowledge of what current models, tools, and integrations can quietly do about it.

It is not a workshop. It is not a slide deck. It is not a forty-five minute call where someone in another time zone tells you to use ChatGPT.

It is one fixed price. It is one written document. It is delivered in two days.

The fixed price is the point. Time-and-materials consulting punishes curiosity. A flat fee makes the question, where is the leverage in this business, a one-line decision rather than a procurement event.

III. The Roadmap

Twelve to eighteen pages, written for one reader.

The Roadmap reads like a memo from a partner who actually went and looked at your business. It is not a template. It cites your tools, your numbers, your team, your friction.

  1. 01

    The current state

    What is happening every week, in plain language. Where time goes. Where leads die. Where revenue leaks.

  2. 02

  3. 03

    The simple ones, written so you can build them this week without me.

  4. 04

    The ones that need real engineering. Sized as Builds, with a price and a timeline, take it or leave it.

  5. 05

    The math

    What the year looks like if you do nothing. What it looks like if you do this.
