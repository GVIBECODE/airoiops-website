The Challenge

What was broken before.

A solo operator at $9k MRR with one employee, growing 30 percent year over year. The problems were not effort or skill. They were everywhere the operator could not be at once.

01

1,000+ leads buried in CRM, no follow-up system

Meta ads pushed leads into a form, the form fed CRM, and that was the end of the workflow. No drip, no SMS cadence, no segmentation by lead age. A year of paid lead-gen sitting cold.

02

Google Ads agency reporting fake conversions

The agency reported 167 conversions at a $54 CPA. The actual count of paying recurring customers from those conversions was 8. Real CAC was $1,112. Most of what the agency called "conversions" were duplicate-counted form fills.

03

Manual bookkeeping eating 3 hours per week

Sweep&Go payments were not flowing into QuickBooks automatically. Receipts piled up in a phone camera roll. Monthly reconciliation was a manual export-and-match job.

04

Idle video backlog, no content cadence

Hours of before/after job footage sitting on phones and SD cards, never edited, never posted. Inconsistent presence on TikTok, Instagram, Google Business, YouTube. Zero compounding social proof from work the operator was already doing.

Forensic Audit · The Hidden Story

$8,983 in paid Google Ads, forensically audited.

Not a screenshot tour. We pulled change history, search terms, ad copy, location data, schedule data, conversion config, auction insights, and 7 months of billing PDFs. This is what 8 months of "performing" actually looked like.

Metric Reported by Agency Actual After Forensic Pull
Conversions 167 conversions @ $54 CPA 8 paying recurring customers @ $1,112 CAC
Channel allocation 76% to Search ("performing") Search converted at $116 / Performance Max at $20
Top-spend ZIP 89109 (Strip) included $1,281 spent there for 4 conversions ($320 each)
Worst keyword Not flagged "pet poop removal", $246 spent, 0 conversions
Campaign structure Two parallel Search campaigns Both bid on the same 15+ keywords (self-cannibalization)
Net effect on ROI "Account is healthy" Negative ROI on every customer acquired for 7 months
Total preventable waste detected

Between 51 and 62 percent of the 8-month $8,983 spend was preventable. That is roughly $4,500 to $5,500 of cash that should have stayed in the business. The Assessment turned this into a documented exhibit, not a hunch.
