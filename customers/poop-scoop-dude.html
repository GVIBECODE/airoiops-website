Executive Summary

The short version, in plain English.

Poop Scoop Dude is a Las Vegas residential service business with 100+ recurring clients. Like most owner-operated SMBs, it ran on goodwill, tribal knowledge, and a duct-tape stack: Sweep&Go for field ops, QuickBooks for accounting, a CRM for messaging, a subpar Zapier automation as the glue, and Google Ads burning cash without clear attribution.

The entire operation was rebuilt around a single AI-orchestrated workflow, delivered in phased waves so each win shipped on its own. The subpar Zapier automation was retired. Sweep&Go's broken QuickBooks integration was replaced with a Make scenario. Auto-replies and language detection now answer every inbound lead in real time. The Google Ads account was audited and paused until the wasted ZIPs could be cut. A live operating dashboard now shows the state of the business in one glance.

The result: a small residential service runs with the operational discipline of a company ten times its size, without adding a single employee.

The Problem

What was broken before.

A 100+ client residential service, growing year over year. The problems were not effort or skill. They were everywhere the operator could not be at once.

01

Disconnected systems with no source of truth

Sweep&Go held jobs. QuickBooks held money. The CRM held leads. Nothing talked to anything cleanly. "How is the business doing right now?" required opening five tabs.

02

A failing native integration

The Sweep&Go to QuickBooks link generated orphaned invoices that never tied back to payments. 24 of them sat unreconciled at the time of cleanup. Books drifted further from reality every month.

03

Duct-tape glue running mission-critical sync

A subpar Zapier automation ran the sync logic between platforms. Billed monthly, broke silently, and nobody but the owner could debug it. A single point of failure with no fallback.

04

Ad spend with no answer

Google Ads spent $8,983 across the prior 8 months. Nobody could say which ZIPs paid back and which didn't. Reported conversions did not match real paying customers.

05

Lead-response gaps after hours

Inbound texts arriving at night sat unanswered until morning. By then the lead had called three competitors. Roughly half the inbound flow was leaking out the bottom of the funnel.

06

SMS landing in spam folders

Outbound texts from the business number were getting filtered before clients saw them. No A2P 10DLC registration, no verified sender. Quotes and reminders silently disappearing.

The cost of doing nothing

When the operator runs on context-switching, growth caps at the operator's bandwidth. The next 100 clients were going to break what the first 100 already strained.

The Approach

Stop the bleeding. Then automate.

Every step was reversible. Every decision was logged. Every automation was live-tested against a real owned phone or email before being armed.

Phase 01

Audit and stabilize

A 30-question operator interview produced a written self-assessment of every workflow, tool, and dependency. The Google Ads account was audited line by line. $4,500+ of waste was identified. The account was paused until structural fixes were made.

Phase 02

Consolidate the stack

The Sweep&Go to QuickBooks native integration was disconnected. 24 orphan invoices were voided cleanly. The subpar Zapier automation was retired. A single Make scenario took over orchestration. One reliable scenario beats five clever ones.

Phase 03

Activate AI where it matters

Auto-replies with English / Spanish language detection went live. SMS sender verification cleared the spam-filter problem. The dashboard went live. AI was placed where humans were the bottleneck, not where AI was fashionable.
