Feature · The Operator's Brief

On the Quiet Discipline of Putting Returns Into Writing.

There is a particular flavour of fatigue that visits the small-business operator at half past nine on a Tuesday evening, after the inbox has refused to thin and the spreadsheets have stopped behaving. It is the fatigue of unmeasured work. The week has had texture but no totals. Effort has been spent, and still the question lingers: where, precisely, did it go?

We built AIROIOPS to answer that question, not in conversation but on paper. The Assessment is the answer. It is a single document, twelve to eighteen pages, written for one operator and one business and never reused. It is delivered in forty-eight hours. Its price is one thousand dollars, and that is the entirety of its price.

“The math is on paper before there is a contract. That is the only mercy we owe an operator.” — from the practice notes

What follows in this volume is the practice in plain view. The pages of the Dossier describe what arrives in your inbox. The pages of Commissioned Work list, without theatre, the engagements you may choose if you would rather we do the building. The Method describes the rhythm of the two days. The Field Report tells you what the Assessment did, recently, in a residential service company in Las Vegas with a thousand neglected leads.

Page 24 · The Dossier

What, exactly, arrives.

Four parts, in sequence. Each is a chapter of the report, not a slide. The whole is an instrument of decision; you read it once and you know what to do on Monday.

No. 01

The Operations Audit

A close reading of your tools, workflows, and data. Each recurring task is named, located, and tagged for AI leverage. Nothing is generalised; everything is yours.

No. 02

Quick Wins, Yours to Ship

Five to seven recommendations, ranked by return and effort. Tools you can subscribe to in an afternoon. Prompts you can paste tonight. Configurations a non-technical operator can complete before dinner.

No. 03

The Commission Menu

An optional list of priced builds for the larger plays, set out without flourish. You are welcome to ignore it. If you prefer to hand off, the floor prices are below and the math has been written.

No. 04

The Ninety-Day Roadmap

A sequenced path from the first Quick Win to a fully leveraged stack. Quarterly milestones. A prioritised backlog. Nothing speculative; only the next move and the move after that.

Page 72 · Queries & Replies

Common questions, briefly answered.

  1. Q. I.

    What is the cost of an AI assessment for a small business?

    One thousand dollars, flat. The figure includes the operations audit, the twelve-to-eighteen page custom report delivered in forty-eight hours, and the twenty-minute walk-through. There are no retainers, no surprise charges, and no ninety-day discovery phase.

  2. Q. II.

    How long does the assessment take?

    Forty-eight hours from the moment the intake is complete. The intake may be filled at leisure, or a forty-five-minute discovery call may be booked. Either way, the roadmap arrives two days later.

  3. Q. III.

    Do you serve only small businesses in Las Vegas?

    The practice is based in Las Vegas and serves operators across Las Vegas, Henderson, North Las Vegas, Summerlin, Paradise, Spring Valley, Enterprise, Centennial Hills, Boulder City, Mesquite, Pahrump, and Indian Springs. The work runs over email and a single short call, and we therefore also serve remote clients anywhere in the United States.

  4. Q. IV.

    What is included in the AI Business Assessment?

    An operations audit, five to seven Quick Wins ranked by return, a transparent commission menu for the larger builds, and a ninety-day roadmap with quarterly milestones. The whole, in twelve to eighteen pages, plus a twenty-minute walk-through.

  5. Q. V.

    Will the recommendations work with the software I already use?

    Yes. Every Quick Win is scoped against your stack: customer-relationship system, email, scheduling, billing, advertising. Builds run inside the tools you already pay for. You retain every credential.

  6. Q. VI.

    Are technical skills required to ship the Quick Wins?

    No. They are selected so that a non-technical operator can configure them in an afternoon. If you can paste a prompt into ChatGPT or click through a Zapier setup, you can ship them. The larger builds, with code or multi-step automation, are the optional commissions.

  7. Q. VII.

    How are the larger builds priced?

    Each carries a floor: $1,500 for a Zapier automation, $2,500 for a Custom GPT, $3,500 for a Speed-to-Lead agent, and $4,500 for a process optimisation. We charge the floor or twenty per cent of projected first-year return, whichever is greater. The Assessment writes that number first.
